# Printing Guide - Remove Headers & Footers

## How to Print Without Browser Watermarks/Timestamps

When you print or export to PDF, browsers add headers and footers by default (URL, date, page numbers). Here's how to disable them:

### Chrome / Edge

1. Click **"üìë Export PDF"** or press `Ctrl+P` (Windows) or `Cmd+P` (Mac)
2. In the print dialog, click **"More settings"**
3. **UNCHECK** the following:
   - ‚òê Headers and footers
   - ‚òê Background graphics (optional)
4. Set **Margins** to "Default" or "Minimum"
5. Click **"Save"** or **"Print"**

**Quick Settings:**
- Destination: Save as PDF
- Pages: All
- Layout: Portrait
- Margins: Default
- Scale: Default (100%)

### Firefox

1. Press `Ctrl+P` (Windows) or `Cmd+P` (Mac)
2. Click **"More settings"** or **"Page Setup"**
3. Go to **"Margins & Header/Footer"** tab
4. Set all dropdowns to **"--blank--"**:
   - Header Left: --blank--
   - Header Center: --blank--
   - Header Right: --blank--
   - Footer Left: --blank--
   - Footer Center: --blank--
   - Footer Right: --blank--
5. Click **"OK"** and then **"Print"**

### Safari (Mac)

1. Press `Cmd+P`
2. Click **"Show Details"** (bottom left)
3. **UNCHECK**: "Print headers and footers"
4. Click **"PDF"** ‚Üí **"Save as PDF"**

## Single Page Resume Tips

### ‚ö†Ô∏è IMPORTANT: Content Length Guidelines

To ensure your resume fits on ONE page when printing:

**Recommended Content Limits:**
- **2 Experience positions** with 2-3 bullets each (max 6 bullets total)
- **1-2 Education entries**
- **1-2 Projects** (or remove projects section)
- **8-15 Skills** (auto-categorized into 3 rows)
- **Summary**: 1-2 sentences (optional)

### If Your Resume Splits Across Pages:

1. **Reduce Content First** (most important!):
   - Remove a project or experience position
   - Cut bullets down to 2 per job
   - Shorten your summary
   - Remove less relevant skills

2. **Check Print Preview**:
   - Always use "Print Preview" before saving
   - Should see exactly 1 page, no blank pages
   - Browser zoom should be 100%

3. **Use the "Compact" Template**:
   - Smaller fonts and tighter spacing
   - Can fit ~15% more content

### Optimal One-Page Layout:

- ‚úÖ 0-5 years experience: 1 page (strict)
- ‚úÖ 5-10 years experience: 1 page preferred, 2 acceptable
- ‚úÖ 10+ years experience: 2 pages acceptable

### Seeing Blank Pages?

**This usually means too much content!**
1. ‚úÇÔ∏è Remove one experience position or project
2. ‚úÇÔ∏è Cut bullet points (keep only the best 2-3 per job)
3. ‚úÇÔ∏è Remove or shorten summary
4. üîÑ Refresh print preview
5. ‚úÖ Should now be 1 clean page

## Troubleshooting

### Problem: Multiple pages with blank space
**Solution**: 
- Use "Compact" template
- Reduce content
- Check print preview scale

### Problem: Text cut off at edges
**Solution**: 
- Increase margins to 0.5 inch
- Check paper size is Letter (8.5 x 11)

### Problem: Seeing "Resume Editor" or URL at top
**Solution**: 
- Turn off "Headers and footers" in print settings
- This is browser behavior, not from the app

### Problem: Date/page number at bottom
**Solution**: 
- Turn off "Headers and footers" in print settings
- Set footer to "--blank--" in Firefox

## Professional PDF Export

For best results:

1. **Fill out all sections completely**
2. **Use AI to polish bullets** (if enabled)
3. **Check Resume Quality panel** for errors
4. **Switch to Clean or Compact template**
5. **Print preview** to verify layout
6. **Disable headers/footers**
7. **Save as PDF** with descriptive filename

## File Naming Convention

Good: `Justin_Chen_Resume_2025.pdf`
Bad: `resume.pdf`

Include:
- Your name
- Document type (Resume/CV)
- Year or month
- Optional: Job title or company
